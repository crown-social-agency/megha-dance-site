The Sacred Art of Bharatanatyam

Bharatanatyam is one of India's most ancient and revered classical dance forms, with origins rooted in the sacred temple traditions of Tamil Nadu. Originally performed by devadasis (women dedicated to temple service), the dance was a form of ritual worship and spiritual storytelling, expressing devotion through movement, music, and gesture.

The word "Bharatanatyam" itself reflects its composite nature: bhava (emotion), raga (melody), tala (rhythm), and natya (drama or dance). Each performance is a multidimensional offering, blending intricate footwork, expressive facial gestures, and symbolic hand movements (mudras) to convey stories from Hindu mythology and epics.

About Megha Sarathy

A Lifelong Journey

My journey with Bharatanatyam began when I was just four years old, learning under Guru Aruna in the Kalakshetra style at Mysore, Karnataka. For over 25 years, I have had the privilege of training with Guru Uma Rao, one of the earliest disciples of the legendary Rukmini Devi Arundale.

Teaching Philosophy

Age has nothing to do with art—only passion matters. I don't teach solely for stage shows or performances. I teach because it brings me joy, and I learn alongside my students. Most importantly, dance should be fun!

Our Philosophy

At Vijayanthi Kalakshetra, we believe classical dance is more than an art form—it is a path to self-discovery, discipline, and deep cultural connection. We cultivate not only strong technique but also profound respect for the spiritual foundations of this dance.
